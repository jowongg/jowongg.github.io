Confounding Tool

Inspiration

During the first week, I explored different software or website that are not intended for design-use such as LucidChart, http://yurafuca.com/, and PowerPoint to experiment how I could create aesthetic and meaningful work under an unusual working environment. I knew that I did not want to work with fragments nor collage(screenshots), but one completed graphic. Out of these programs, I decided to work with LucidChart for my project. LucidChart enables me to have a high level of freedom and control over creating graphics, yet some limitations (e.g. arrangement of lines between bubbles) on design, leading to an interesting visual effect that could only be made with this software.

Planning &

In the second week, I had a clear intention on the purpose of my work, which is illustrating my struggle to reply to an overwhelming number of messages under the pandemic. The red bubbles in the design represent notification. They are formed as a cluster representing the tension and stress when I don't reply to messages and just let them pile up. I made little pixel black hands in http://pixelartmaker.com/ and added to the composition just to vary the visual elements as well as enhancing the tension.

Advices

Feedback from classmates/ professor:

  • Play with the number of hands/ less hands/no hand - suggestion is taken with removing the elements of hands
  • Different shapes of hands
  • Add in something that relates to messages or notification - suggestion is taken with adding in “Do not disturb” bubble
  • Play with dimension (scale)
  • Floating red bubbles outside of cluster - suggestion is taken with creating floating bubbles (during experimental stage)
  • Bold/black lines? - suggestion is taken with making the lines bold and black

Experiment &

During the third week, I had created different compositions without changing the main structure of the cluster. I added a ”Do not disturb” bubble in the middle of the cluster for most of the experimental composition to vary the message and enhance visual connotation. It allows the audience to understand that red bubbles are representing notification, as well as creating room for interpretation of my design. For the third one, I experimented with typographic-alike composition, but most of the comments said the text behind the cluster was somewhat distracting. Therefore, I chose the first composition to develop instead of the third one.

Advices

Feedback from classmates/ professor:

  • Big black text are distracting
  • uniting black lines in the middle of the cluster to create a sucking effect
  • first and third compositions could be developed further for final - suggestion is taken with developing the first composition as final design
